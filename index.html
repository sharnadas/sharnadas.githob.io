About Me

    B.SC.Thesis


    Detecting skin cancer through the utilization of various types of Deep Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)

    Under Review
    Advisor: Mr. M M Fazle Rabbi(Assistant Professor, Department of Computer Science & Engineering,(BUBT))

    Abstract: Skin cancer is a type of cancer that develops within the skin. In the last several years, deep learning has been applied extensively to improve the diagnosis accuracy in medical imaging tasks such as segmentation and classification. Advanced deep-learning algorithms use layers of neural networks to extract important visual qualities and picture information. The learning process became more challenging as more layers were added. In recent years, artificial intelligence (AI) and deep learning techniques have shown promising potential for revolutionizing skin cancer detection. To detect skin cancer, we propose convolutional neural networks (CNNs) with generative adversarial network (GAN)- based augmentation. Generative Adversarial Networks have been utilized to increase the model diagnostic capability, augment data, and prevent overfitting. International Skin Imaging Collaboration (ISIC) 2020 dataset was used to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed deep- learning model. The performance of the first proposed model was observed with 93.36 percent accuracy using a limited dataset. The final proposed method utilized both traditional and GAN-based augmentation techniques to enlarge the training dataset. It performed better with a performance accuracy of 95.01 percent and better data validation. Consequently, curing the deadliest type of skin cancer is a positive service for humanity.
